Statement with Debbie Rowe on Media Reports

 

“Herald Sun” (1997)

An angry and disappointed Michael Jackson yesterday hit back at detractors who he said had dogged his world HIStory tour.

In a bid to staunch the flow of unfavorable publicity surrounding his November [15] Sydney marriage to nurse Deborah Rowe and her pregnancy, Jackson issued a statement in Los Angeles yesterday.

In it, his wife spoke for the first time about the pain caused by publicity surrounding her pregnancy, particularly what she said were false statements attributed to her father.

"I have been on cloud nine since the wedding", Ms. Rowe-Jackson said.

"My happiness has been marred only by the inconsiderate, hurtful, and untruthful things that have been said about Michael and me by the media."

MTV News (January 8, 1997)

On Monday, two days after he performed his only scheduled U.S. concert in Honolulu, Hawaii, Michael Jackson's publicists issued a statement saying the singer is "being distracted and distressed by false media reports full of fabrications and speculations."

Albuquerque News  (January 24, 1997)

The singer "regrets that while he is on an exciting concert tour, he is being distracted and distressed by so many false media reports full of fabrications and speculations," his statement reads. "Michael and Deborah are looking forward with great anticipation to the arrival of their baby. “Herald Sun” (1997) (continued)

"Deborah has been extremely upset by the flagrant fabrications and utter lies that have been published about her and the false statements attributed to her father which he never made.

"Deborah's father supports her decisions 100 percent", the statement said, referring to reports he didn't approve of her marriage.

Ms. Rowe-Jackson repeated that:

Jackson is the father of the child.

The pregnancy is not the result of artificial means.

She has not been paid to have Jackson's baby.

She is not seeking or filing for a divorce.
