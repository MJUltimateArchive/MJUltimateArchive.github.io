"The Three Tenors" Charity Concert Attendance

Date confirmed in “Associated Press” (“Tuesday [June 17, 1997] night”)

 

“Associated Press” (June 18, 1997) (archived)

The "Three Tenors" performed to a sell-out crowd Tuesday night in a charity concert in the Italian city of Modena to raise money to rebuild two opera houses.

Luciano Pavarotti teamed with Spaniards Placido Domingo and Jose Carreras before a crowd of 22-thousand at the Braglia soccer stadium.

The audience included American rock star Michael Jackson and movie director Franco Zeffirelli who paid up to 600-thousand lire (353 U-S dollars) for front-row seats.

The three tenors are back.

This time they performed to a crowd of 22-thousand in Modena -- Luciano Pavarotti hometown.

The concert kicked off "Pavarotti International Week" -- a tribute to the tenor who has provided an easy way into opera for millions around the world.

The Modena event includes a major horse show with Olympic and world champions.

Horses are one of Pavarotti's passions.

The televised concert raised about 3.2 billion lire (1.8 million US dollars) for reconstruction funds for two opera houses: the La Fenice theater in Venice and Barcelona's Grand Teatre del Liceu.

Both were badly damaged by fire.

The tenors teamed up with New York's Metropolitan Opera Orchestra, under the baton of conductor James Levine, the Met's artistic director.

...Jackson -- who spent the day holed up in his 2-thousand U-S dollars a night hotel suite dodging fans -- has a concert Wednesday at a Milan soccer stadium.

 

Daniele Nannuzzi, friend of film director Franco Zeffirelli, “Julien’s Auctions” listing (November 27, 2009) (archived)

My name is Daniele Nannuzzi, close friend and business partner of Franco Zeffirelli, a very famous movies director in Italy.

In 1997 on day 17th [of June], we had the pleasure to meet backstage Mr. Michael Jackson just before the beginning of "The 3 Tenors" show in Modena, performed by Luciano Pavarotti, Jose Carreras, and Placido Domingo; we sat in first row, and Mr. Jackson was next to Mr. Zeffirelli and Mrs. Nicoletta Mantovani, Mr. Pavarotti's wife.

After the show, we all met backstage, where Mr. Pavarotti went out with the idea to organize an event to fundraising the Italian Cancer Foundation: Mr. Jackson wanted to be part of it, telling us he was going to send some private items as soon as he returned to Milan, where the next day he was going to perform a live show.

On day 19th [of June], we received this wonderful pair of shoes Mr. Jackson wore on stage in Milano on day 18th, along with a black fedora signed as well.

Unfortunately, the event never took place...
