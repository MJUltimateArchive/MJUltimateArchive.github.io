"Who Is It" Music Video / Short Film

Note: There are two music videos for “Who Is It”, one directed by David Fincher released on July 13, 1992, and a compilation video by Glenn Ribble, released in March 1993.

Date range of filming assessed by the fact that the parts Michael was in were filmed shortly before Michael went on tour (“Michael Jackson was on tour at the time, and a double was used for the location shots from the balcony of the hotel.”)

 

David Fincher, director, Rolling Stone (June 24, 2014)

Before becoming an Oscar-nominated filmmaker, David Fincher was one of the most in-demand directors of the MTV era. His collaboration with Jackson foretells the elegant, sinister, uncompromising style he'd bring to his movies. The gorgeous video, in which Michael falls for a high-priced escort and stares sadly at the city skyline, survives on video anthologies and has racked up 6 million YouTube plays. "To me it was very simple: Either we agree on what we're gonna do or we don't," said Fincher about working with musicians. "But, I'm not gonna trick you into doing something you don't wanna do."

 

Glenn Ribble, MTV producer, personal blog post (February 12, 2014) (archived)

David Fincher had directed a high-budget video for “Who Is It” in Brazil for the release of the single on August 31, 1992. Michael Jackson was on tour at the time, and a double was used for the location shots from the balcony of the hotel. Michael rejected Fincher’s video; he felt that it didn’t accurately represent the song. Needless to say, David Fincher was enraged when my version [(compilation video)] appeared on MTV with images from Michael’s previous videos, many of which Fincher had shot, with me listed as the director. The other directors and labels were upset as well.

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

It’s believed Michael walked out after the first day of filming, after becoming disappointed with Fincher’s overall approach to the shoot. This may explain why Michael was never happy with it. A body double was used in some scenes to complete the video.
