"Give In To Me" Music Video / Short Film

Date confirmed in Adrian Grant’s Visual Documentary and the statement on this TV broadcast that “It was filmed in Munich prior to the first live show [(June 27, 1992])”

 

Michael Jackson, Oprah Interview, 1993

Slash, who's a dear friend of mine, we love animals and things like that, he wanted to play guitar and I wanted him to play guitar. We got together and we went to Germany and we shot this thing in just like two hours. We had no time at all to shoot it. We wanted it to be exciting and fantastical and fans, you know, like it's a rock concert and that's how it ends up-that's the result.

 

Daniel Pearl, cinematographer, "Movie Geeks United" (June 28, 2009)

Daniel Pearl: Give In To Me we shot in Munich, Germany, Michael was preparing to go on a big European tour, so he was already in Germany rehearsing. Andy Morahan was the director... We shot that in an abandoned railway repair factory, and they had big turntables where you could drive an old steam locomotive onto this track, and the track could rotate 180 or 360 to turn around and get the train back out. And there were a lot of different heights and levels to this place where Andy had us build this steamy, kind of sex-oriented club, not over the top, nothing too bad. [The audience] sorta has a little bit of a dark side to it in my mind, and I think it's also very interesting cuz it's a little darker than Michael was going at that time too... What we weren't aware of was that there were Russians there, I don't know if the Iron Curtain was just down around this time, they were bringing in cowhides by the truckload  which they were tanning there, which had this outrageous odor that permeated the whole building. They actually installed a perfume system throughout the building for the time-period that Michael would be in there, and we were working there, that one we had a 2-day shoot, we had only 7 hours with Michael. So we pre-lit, we lit it very carefully... Those of you who have seen it, I thought I did some interesting things, I had some new lights that I'd never seen before, some programmable small Xenons that would actually be remote-controlled by a computer. I had, I think, eight cameramen, eight operators working for me at that time with the director, and I had just finished everything, got it all tweaked up and I was showing the director who was setup at the back of this massive room we were in with his eight monitors, showing him all the various cameras and we were playing the track and we probably had a stand-in in for Michael and we were running the lights... Michael's manager, at the time, was sitting with Andy. And so, when [Andy] was done, he goes, "Daniel, it look fantastic, it's really good now. As soon as I have a word with Michael, we'll start shooting. You're ready, right?"

I said, "Yeah, I'm ready. Let's go."

So the manager goes, "Oh no, Andy, I'm sorry, but you're not gonna get to speak with Michael."

Andy goes, "Are you crazy, man? Daniel, get ready to take it all down."

Now this is a million plus [dollars] video... I'm told to be on stand-by and ready to give orders to strike the whole thing, we're not gonna shoot it. The manager goes, "What are you talking about?"

Andy goes, "No, no, under no circumstances will I shoot this video until I have spoken to Michael Jackson."

He goes, "Alright, alright, alright. I can see you're serious about that. Hang on a second."

The manager gets up, he goes away to go see Michael at his - he had a quadrangle of RVs, four of them that were setup with one way in and out that lead right into our building. And I walked over to Andy and I said, "Andy, are you kidding, man? ...I mean this would be gigantic if we don't shoot this. There's gonna be lawsuits, all kinds of shit will go down."

He goes, "Daniel, the rest of my life, people are gonna ask me, 'What was it like to work with Michael Jackson?' What am I gonna say, 'I don't know'?"

And the manager came back, he says, "Ok, Andy, come right this way, Michael will have a word with you."

Andy went away for about 10 - 15 minutes, came back, he said,

"Alright guys, I've spoken to him, I told him what I wanted him to do. We're good to go."

And we went and we shot it.

Movie Geeks United: Was there much conversation between Michael and the directors on these shoots typically?

Daniel Pearl: No, in fact, going into "Give In To Me", we were all told that "you will not speak to Michael Jackson, you will not look at Michael Jackson, and for God's sake, you will not take his picture."... I was allowed to look at him, I was allowed to take his picture. The assistant director, a very dear friend of mine as well, a man named Ian Patrick, he was allowed to speak to him to tell him "We're ready" [and] to bring him to and from the stage, but was really told not to look at him.

Anyhow, so we went and we did it. We got it done, and it turned out pretty well, I think. I think even Michael was happy with it.

***Footnotes***

The “abandoned railway repair factory” was located at 166 Landsberger Straße, Munich, Germany, according to Adrian Grant’s Visual Documentary.
