London, England (Dangerous World Tour)

Date confirmed in Adrian Grant’s “Michael Jackson: A Visual Documentary”

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

On 1st August, 1992 as fans waited patiently for his arrival on stage at Wembley Stadium it was suddenly announced that the show was cancelled due to Michael falling ill. In a statement released shortly afterwards Vice President of communications Bob Jones said:

“Michael Jackson is suffering from virus type symptoms and he has decided to cancel the show. He had planned to perform but it is a nausea-type virus and on the advice of his physicians it was decided to cancel. Obviously he is very upset.

“Michael loves to perform and is in heaven when he is performing for his fans. He is happiest on stage giving his all. This is not a decision we are happy to announce.”

A few days later from the Dorchester Hotel Michael threw a white napkin from his balcony for his fans with a message to them. It read:

“I love you all very much. I’m feeling better now. Thank you for your compassion. MJ”

Later he had to leave a Science Museum quickly after a hoax bomb call.
