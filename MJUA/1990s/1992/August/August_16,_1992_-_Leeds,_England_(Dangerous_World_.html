Leeds, England (Dangerous World Tour)

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

At Leeds Roundhay Park backstage before his show, he met youngster Nicholas Killen whose parents house was ruined in a £10,000 blaze destroying the 6 years old’s toys and a cap Michael had given him at an earlier date. When the Daily Express contacted Michael’s management and told him what had happened he made arrangements to meet him again to present him with two caps. Nicholas had been diagnosed with cancer and had to have both eyes removed to stop cancer spreading.

Speaking about his new caps later the delighted youngster said:

“They’re great. I knew Michael was my friend and I want to thank him very much”
