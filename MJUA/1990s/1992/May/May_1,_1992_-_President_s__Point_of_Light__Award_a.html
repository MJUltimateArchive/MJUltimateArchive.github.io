President's "Point of Light" Award at the White House

Date confirmed in Deseret News (“Friday”, May 1, 1992)

 

Michael Jackson, speech transcript

I wanted to come to lunch, because I believe each person can make a difference in the life of someone in need. That is what a Point Of Light is. I'm very, very happy to be here. I love you all. Thank you very much.

 

Deseret News (May 2, 1992)

Now you can call him Ambassador Michael Jackson.

The singer was recognized by President Bush on Friday as "a points of light ambassador" for such kindnesses as inviting disadvantaged young people and children with disabilities to his California ranch.Jackson spoke briefly at an East Room ceremony in which the president honored 21 winners of the 1992 President's Annual Points of Light Awards, a culmination of National Volunteer Week.

"I know each person can make a difference in the life of someone in need," Jackson said. "I love you all."

President Bush told the honorees that "for all the good that government can do - and it can do some good - to solve our country's social problems, we need people, we need every individual to respond."

Service to others, he said, "is a rich source of meaning in life."

Jackson goes on a list of other show business ambassadors such as Anita Baker, Tony Danza, Lou Gossett Jr., Mike Love, the Beach Boys, Sandy Patti and Randy Travis.

 

Unknown newspaper snippet

President George Bush honored superstar Michael Jackson and 20 other Americans for special contributions to volunteer work.

The president, who recognized Jackson for his work with disadvantaged and disabled children, lunched with the singer and gave him a seat of honor at the front of the White House East Room during the awards ceremony yesterday.

They were joined at the ceremony by volunteers from nursing homes, tutoring programs, day-care centers, and anti-drug projects from around the United States.

Jackson, sporting an armband, a studded leather belt and long curls, did not sing to the group, but he did make a brief statement in which he declared: "I love you all."
