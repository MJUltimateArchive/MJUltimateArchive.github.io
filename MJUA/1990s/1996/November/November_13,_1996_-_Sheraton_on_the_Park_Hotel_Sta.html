“Herald Sun” (November 14, 1996)

An emotional Michael Jackson last night vowed to visit the six-year-old burns victim who has captured the hearts of Australia.

Jackson was so moved by the plight of tiny Tjandamurra O'Shane, who last month was doused in petrol and set alight in the grounds or a Cairns primary school. that he will pull out all stops to visit the youngster.

Sources said the pop superstar learned about the attack from news, papers on his first day in Australia.

He became emotional When a visit to the Child's hospital bedside was proposed late yesterday.

Jackson immediately agreed, telling his minders to fit the visit into his hectic tour schedule.

Tjandamurra received serious burns to 70 per cent of his body, but is responding well to skin grafts.

A 26-year-old Adelaide man has been charged over the attack.

Jackson's empathy for the youngster probably stems from his own horrific burns experience.

In 1984, Jackson's hair caught fire on the set of a commercial the singer was shooting. He received serious scalp burns.

...In Sydney yesterday, the reclusive superstar stayed inside his $3500-a-night luxury suite watching a selection of videos, including action adventure and Australian titles.

...Sweltering 35 degree [(95 degrees Fahrenheit)] heat did not deter about 50 diehard fans and media crews from staking out his hotel.

Inside, Jackson was said to be enjoying the most expensive hotel room in Australia, complete with grand piano, a dance floor, a mini bar stocked with soft drinks, and catering requests for fresh salads, and fruit.

...The woman who is carrying Jackson's baby was ushered quietly into Sydney last night.

Debbie Rowe, 37, sneaked into the harbor city unnoticed, checking into the hotel where Jackson is staying about 6:30pm.

Dressed casually, and looking tired from her flight, Ms. Rowe was whisked to the top floors of the Sheraton on the Park Hotel, to join Jackson and his entourage.
