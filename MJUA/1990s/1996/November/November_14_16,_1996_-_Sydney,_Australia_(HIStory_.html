Sydney, Australia (HIStory World Tour)

 

“Herald Sun” (1996)

If the mask is a metaphor for silence, then superstar Michael Jackson took it off--and left it off--for a sell-out crowd of 45,000 in Sydney last night.

Jackson's first Australian show in nine years was a 2 1/2-hour thriller that recreated his music, his artistry, his dance genius: HIStory.

...From the hi-tech, virtual reality intro, where he crash lands a space module into the stadium, it is a non-stop ride of the man and his music.

Highlights are many and varied, from the sincerity in "Earth Song" to the inspired magic of "Billie Jean", Jackson's HIStory show has got it all.

An obviously buoyant Jackson told the crowd many times that he loved them, and also introduced different brackets in his set with a smile.

"I would like to do some of my favorite song. Hopefully they're some of yours as well", Jackson said before leaping into a medley of tracks from "Off the Wall".

...Earlier, Jackson abandoned plans to visit Australia's pluckiest kid because of fears the child is too ill for all the attention.

An emotional Jackson had planned to visit six-year-old Tjandamurra O'Shane--who was doused with petrol and set alight in the grounds of a Cairns school last month--but the child's father, Timothy O'Shane, warned against it.

Mr. O'Shane told Jackson's minders that his son was too sick to deal with the media circus following the singer.

Management at the Royal Children's Hospital in Brisbane said it was also concerned about the effect on Tjandamurra's slow but steady progress.

Jackson said he respected the family's wishes.

However, Jackson has sent his best wishes to Tjandamurra in the form of a personalized card, an autographed copy of his HIStory CD, posters, flowers, and other gifts.

Sourced say Jackson's card wishes Tjandamurra a "speedy recovery" and that the superstar finds the youngster's long road to recovery "brave and inspiring."

Yesterday, in day two of Jackson-watch, it is believed the singer and several members of his entourage, did an impromptu early morning tour though Sydney.

Jackson was said to have left his $3500-a-night suite at 4am, and drive across the Harborough Bridge to the site of Luna Park and back to the Opera House.

"People Weekly" magazine

When Debbie Rowe ambled into Sydney airport after a 14-hour flight from Los Angeles last Wednesday, few took note of what was, in hindsight, a historic moment. Already six months pregnant with Michael Jackson's child, Rowe, 37, would soon go from being an anonymous dermatologist's aide to the Peter Pan of Pop's blushing bride.

The marriage, kept secret even from some of his entourage, took place after the star performed in his brilliantly choreographed concert before 40,000 fans at the Sydney Cricket Ground last Thursday night.

After the sell-out show... Jackson returned to the Sheraton on the Park hotel. "He went up the steps and stood and waved to the media", says a spokeswoman for promoter Paul Dainty. "He had his mask on and said everything with those wonderful eyes."

Then he disappeared inside, where a cocktail party was in full swing. Attended by the power-suits of the local rock industry, including Molly Meldrum, Brian Walsh (Australian publicist for Jackson) and some of the singer's 200-strong entourage, it lasted until about 3 AM. "Everybody arrived from the concert", a Sheraton employee told Sydney's 2Day FM, "and they had all been very merry, they had been drinking all night."

Jackson's bride-to-be, who miscarried earlier this year after she and Jackson first conceived, was hidden from view. "I'd say she was preparing for the wedding", said record marketing director Stephen Millard. "She's a very nice girl", adds Dainty's spokeswoman. "It's weird--she's normal."

...With Jackson's personal adviser, Charles Bobbit, and world tour promoter Marcel Avram standing by, Jackson and Rowe were married just after midnight in the Sheraton's Presidential Suite. The bride, says Brian Walsh, "wore white. I know that Michael's hair and make-up people attended to Debbie prior to the wedding."

"They are really happy and Michael's ecstatic about the prospects of being a father--and now he has the wife as well", says Millard. Rowe, he adds, is "very relaxed. She's a really gentle person, very genuine, and very loving." A friend of Rowe's agrees. "She's the antithesis of Lisa Marie--she never wears lipstick, never gets her hair done, drives a pick-up truck." And that, according to a source close to Jackson, is exactly what the singer wants. "There's no question Michael wants this child to have a mother and father--that's why he got married."

But whether it is a match made in Hollywood heaven or not, Jackson still wants Hollywood heaven or not, Jackson still wants "Debbie to see Sydney", says Dainty's spokeswoman, but "he won't go out with her." Jackson, however, has insisted he see the city and the people "he absolutely loves", says Millard. "He thinks there's more of an understanding here of him than anywhere." Between daytime rests at his hotel, Jackson plans a trip to the zoo, and while in Brisbane this week will visit Tjandamurra O'Shane, the 6-year-old torched in a Caims schoolyard a month ago. Jackson has also asked Sony for a $399 CD-ROM PlayStation as a gift for the child.

"Sydney Morning Herald" (1996)

At the SCG, an even greater journey was taken in a show where understatement was replaced by grand gestures.

Before Michael Jackson even made it to the stage, a computer game-like rollercoaster ride through history fractured shots of major figures of the past and present, interspersed with shots of Jackson. He then emerged from a capsule in a gold space suit and launched into his newish song "Scream".

After 10 minutes, he unzipped his jacket. Five minutes later, he disappeared. By the time he returned, sex had arrived via security clad women.

Jackson wielded a machine gun. Sex, violence, and a moonwalk... he had it all.

 

Marva Hicks, “HIStory World Tour” “I Just Can’t Stop Loving You” duet singer and backup singer, “L.A. Watts Times” (June 24, 2010) (archived)

My fondest memory of MJ is when my mother became really ill and (was) in intensive care. We were performing in Sydney, Australia. At that evening’s performance on the day that I found out she was sick, MJ pulled me aside after our prayer circle. He always joined us for prayer circle. He thanked me for doing the show that night considering how worried I was about my mother. And he assured me they would get me home ASAP. I left on a flight the next morning. And the next day, flowers arrived at the hospital for my mother — from Michael Jackson. I am loyal to him to this day because of his kindness. What I [appreciate] most about him is the dynamics of his creativity. Being around him was electric! It was exciting to be a part of building this show.
