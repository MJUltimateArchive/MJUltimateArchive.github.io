"ATV Music" Merge with "Sony Music Publishing" / "Words Without Meaning"

Date confirmed in “UPI” (archived) (“Michael Jackson and Sony announced Tuesday [November 7, 1995]”) / Date of “Words Without Meaning” confirmed in 3T’s “Brotherhood” album release date (November 7, 1995).

 

"Jet" magazine (November 27, 1995)

Megastar Michael Jackson has merged his music catalog with Sony Corp.'s music publishing division in a joint venture reportedly valued at $600 million.

Jackson’s music catalog, ATV Music Publishing, includes some 250 Beatles songs as well as songs associated with Elvis Presley and Little Richard.

Sony/ATV Music Publishing will include all of the current and future music publishing interests of both parties.

The Sony catalog includes songs by such Sony recording artists as Mariah Carey, Sade, Bob Dylan and Neil Diamond.

Jackson's music catalog includes such Beatles tunes as Come Together, We Can Work It Out, Hey Jude and Yesterday.

Jackson’s own songs were not included in the deal, Sony said.

Jackson said in a press statement: “The merging of ATV with Sony establishes our commitment to create one of the largest music publishing ventures in the world. We have been working on this for over a year and, now, with the two of us together, the sky is our only limit.”

Jackson denied rumors that he made the merger deal with Sony be qcause he is having financial problems during an interview with USA Today. “This acquisition had nothing to do with needing funds,” Jackson said. “It’s just a great move, a corporate, entrepreneurial thing to do. It’s smart business. It's about growth. Everything in

life to me is about growth.”

Michael P. Schulhof, president and CEO, Sony Corporation of America, said: “Michael Jackson is not only the most successful entertainer in history; he is also an astute businessman. Michael understands the importance of copyrights and the role they play in the introduction of new technologies. In joining us for this new venture, Michael recognizes Sony’s leadership in developing and realizing new technologies that serve to expand the creative horizons of artists such as himself.”

Sony Music Entertainment President Tommy Mottola, who is married to Grammy Award winner Mariah Carey, said, “Today, Sony Music takes its rightful place once again as a major force in music publishing. Copyrights and songs are the foundation of every- thing in our entire industry.”

He added, “To have Michael Jackson's song catalogs joined with ours will enable both Sony Music and Michael Jackson to aggressively build on a publishing portfolio filled with songs that are acknowledged as the cornerstones of popular music today.”

Sony said its new catalog will be the third biggest in music publishing. The largest is Time Warner Inc.'s Warner-Chappell, followed by EMI Music Publishing, according to news reports. Music publishing catalogs generate revenues through compact disc and tape sales and recorded and live performances.

Jackson bought ATV in 1985 for about $47.5 million. Other bidders at the time included ex-Beatle Paul McCartney.

Financial terms of the new venture were not publicly disclosed at JET press time.

 

“UPI” (November 7, 1995) (archived)

Michael Jackson and Sony announced Tuesday they are compiling the current and future song catalogs of the pop star's ATV Music Publishing and Sony Music Publishing in a joint venture to be called Sony/ATV Music Publishing. The joint venture, with combined assets of about $500 million according to Sony, includes 251 Beatles tunes as well as songs by Mariah Carey, Willie Nelson and Babyface, but Jackson's own compositions remain separate in his MIJAC catalog maintained by Warner Chappell. Jackson's share of the deal was not disclosed, but reports said Jackson sought $100 million and settled for $90 million. A published report this week said the self-proclaimed king of pop is relatively low on cash because he has paid huge settlements to parents of boys who have accused him of molestation, while maintaining his lavish lifestyle. 'The merging of ATV with Sony establishes our commitment to create one of the largest music publishing ventures in the world,' Jackson said in the announcement. 'We have been working on this for over a year and, now, with the two of us together, the sky is our only limit.' Jackson's role in Sony/ATV was not specified, but a representative of his MJJ Productions said, 'He will be active in a decision-making capacity.' Michael P. Schulhof, president and chief executive officer of Sony Corp. of America, said the joint venture is an extension of Sony's 'very special relationship' with Jackson. 'Michael Jackson is not only the most successful entertainer in history; he is also an astute businessman,' Schulhof said.
