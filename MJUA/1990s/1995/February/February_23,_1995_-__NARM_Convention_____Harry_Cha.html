"NARM Convention" / "Harry Chapin Memorial Humanitarian Award" Acceptance

Date confirmed in “The San Diego Union-Tribune” (archived) (“yesterday [February 23, 1995]”)

 

“The San Diego Union-Tribune” (February 24, 1995) (archived)

The ongoing rehabilitation of Michael Jackson’s tarnished public image continued yesterday afternoon when the pop superstar appeared at the San Diego Convention Center to receive a humanitarian award and promote his forthcoming album.

It was his first public appearance since he and his wife, Lisa Marie Presley, appeared at the MTV Music Video Awards last September in New York.

Jackson was presented with the Harry Chapin Memorial Humanitarian Award, named after the socially conscious folk singer, who died in 1981.

Jackson was given a standing ovation by a crowd of more than 2,000 at the 37th annual National Association of Recording Merchandisers (NARM) convention.

The award was presented by Scott Young, president of NARM, who hailed Jackson’s “extraordinary efforts to enhance the quality of life on this planet.”

...The singer, who is usually shy in public when not performing on stage, smiled broadly as he thanked the audience. “I’m honored and I’m humbled with your acknowledging my efforts,” he said, after becoming only the fifth recipient of the award in the past decade. “I sincerely appreciate your true love and support.”

Jackson was dressed in black pants, an elaborate red shirt with epaulets, and aviator sunglasses. His hair was tied back and he appeared healthy and in good spirits.

His unbilled appearance came at the conclusion of a promotional program by his record company, Epic, which previewed five new songs from the singer’s long-overdue new album “HIStory Book I.”

Now scheduled for a spring release, the 150-minute double album will consist of a greatest-hits disc and a disc of new material. Prior to his appearance, excerpts were played from the five new songs -- “Money,” “Stranger in Moscow,” “They Don’t Care About Us,” “Earth Song” and “Scream,” a high-powered duet with his sister Janet.

Jackson, who usually limits his public speaking to a sentence or two at best, addressed the audience for nearly two minutes. Most of his comments were about his forthcoming album.

“For over 20 years now we have created magic (and) history in breaking new ground, which is something we will also do with `HIStory Book I.’ My heartfelt thanks to my Epic (Records) family . . . you guys really know how to make things happen.

“I appreciate this very much. All my love. Thank you very much.”

As the crowd applauded, Epic honcho David Glew whispered in the singer’s ear. "(He) just told me to tell you something about the new album,” Jackson said. “I don’t know what to say.”

Before the singer could continue, Glew grabbed the microphone from him and declared that the still-uncompleted album was “absolutely amazing.”

While Jackson’s Convention Center appearance occurred under heavy security, he earlier spent several hours at a downtown San Diego bookstore, where he browsed for nearly two hours and bought several boxes of mostly out-of-print books. Jackson, who wore a red satin surgical mask, was accompanied by several bodyguards and by his wife, who did not appear at the Convention Center gathering.
