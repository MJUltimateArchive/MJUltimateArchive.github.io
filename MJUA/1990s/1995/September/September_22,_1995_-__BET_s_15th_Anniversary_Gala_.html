"BET's 15th Anniversary Gala"

Date confirmed in “Chicago Tribune” (archived) (“Filmed before a live studio audience on Sept. 22”) and on the special plaque Michael receives (“September 22, 1995”)

 

“Associated Press” (September 23, 1995) (archived)

Michael Jackson has been honoured by Black Entertainment Television in America as the most significant performer in the music video industry.

The celebrated singer performed his current hit "You are not Alone", as part of a televised celebration in Washington for the cable network's 15th anniversary.

Black Entertainment Television, which features many music video shows as well as current affair programmes, hosted a variety of celebrities for its anniversary party.

From big political names like Democrats Ron Brown and Jesse Jackson, to entertainers like Tim Reid - dozens of famous black Americans came out for Friday's bash.

And, of course, there was tight security around Michael Jackson as he arrived.

A series of comedians and singers preceded Jackson's performance.

Peabo Bryson and Regina Belle sang "A Whole New World", from the movie "Aladdin".

Jackson, with his now trademark dramatic, smoky entrance, treated the audience to his latest top ten hit, "You are Not Alone".

He was accompanied by the Union Temple Baptist Choir of Washington.

The party continued until the small hours, with selected guests joining celebrities for a buffet and dancing afterwards.

The presentation of the award, and star on the network's Walk of Fame, concluded the festivities.

 

“Chicago Tribune” (December 6, 1995) (archived)

"BET's 15th Anniversary Gala": Cable's Black Entertainment Television offers up this salute to itself at 8 tonight, in honor of its decade-and-a-half as the only national network that targets an African-American audience. Filmed before a live studio audience on Sept. 22 at the network's new Washington, D.C., production facility, the concert features performances by Michael Jackson (singing "You Are Not Alone"), Peabo Bryson (singing "A Whole New World" in a duet with Regina Belle), and Gerald and Eddie Levert singing together.
