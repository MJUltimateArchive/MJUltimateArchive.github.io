"Triumph" Album Recording

Date range assessed by the fact that, according to studio engineer Tom Perry, the “Triumph” album was a “six month period”; they finished the music of all tracks before starting vocals, so the recording was likely to be the latter six months of the sessions (sessions began in late June 1979) after the December 21, 1979 concert on their Destiny Tour. “We would start to do some guitar, keyboard, and synth overdubs. Then, we would go onto the next song. Generally, we would work on one song per day working on rhythm tracks and we wouldn’t get into recording any serious vocals, background vocals, strings, or horns until later.” (“Wax Poetics”) (archived)

 

“Keyboard” magazine (July 2012) (archived)

“Keyboard”: How did your work with Michael Jackson develop after that?

Phillinganes: I had worked on the Jackson 5’s Triumph album after Destiny. After that, Quincy asked me to be involved in Michael’s solo album Off the Wall. I played on virtually all of that album, and things just took off from there.
