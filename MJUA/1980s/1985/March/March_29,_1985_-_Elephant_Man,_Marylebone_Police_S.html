Elephant Man, Marylebone Police Station, and Platinum Award

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

Michael visited the hospital to view the ‘Elephant Man’ John Merrick’s skeleton, the hood he wore over his head and a small model of a church Merrick made.

That same day, he made an afternoon visit to Marylebone Police station, a request he’d made to his management to arrange. He was given a tour of the station by an Inspector Beer, who showed him, the cells, the communications system and the shoplifting unit. He was also given the chance to view a police lineup with notebooks and truncheons.

Beer said: “I just asked him about his plans and what he thought about the sights of London. He seemed a very ordinary sort of chap.”

At an evening reception the same day Michael was presented with his award for Thriller going Platinum eight times.

 

“No.1” magazine (1985)

It is Friday, March 29, 1985, the day after Michael Jackson's appearance at Madame Tussaud's. On this bright, clear afternoon, he dodges a knot of photographers and fans outside the Montcalm Hotel to pay a visit to the London Hospital. For the second time in two years, he's off to view the skeleton of The Elephant Man.

Scarcely has Michael's black limousine left the hotel precincts then it is surrounded by a knot of screaming, yelling fans.

They paw the car and one twists off the quarterlight. Michael, cowering in the back, quickly pulls a coat up over his head.

"Are you frightened of them, Michael?", asks a British aide as the car pulls clear.

Michael uncovers his face and looks pensive. "Onstage", he says, "I'm a hero. But offstage, I don't think I'm the star they want me to be..."

Ironic words. Because the whole of Michael's British visit shows him at last trying to be just the star the fans want.

An accessible one.

He is deliberately trying to destroy the image of Jackson the recluse…

…Back To Whitechapel

Friday March 29. Michael's lunchtime visit to the London Hospital is his second in two years.

En route, he fires questions about various landmarks such as Cleopatra's Needle, the date when the Houses of Parliament were built and whose statue is in the courtyard. (It's Oliver Cromwell's.)

"What's that place? It looks like a palace", he asks, pointing at the GLC's County Hall headquarters. And he listens with interest as an aide explains exactly what the Greater London Council is.

In a private teaching section of the London Hospital, six or seven students are busy revising when a familiar figure enters.

The section which contains the skeleton of Elephant Man John Merrick, also has a number of other exhibits including jars containing pickled lungs and livers. But Michael's only interested in Merrick's skeleton, the famous hood that he wore over his head, and a small model of a church which Merrick made.

He spends just five minutes in the hospital. On the way to the car outside, a group of local Whitechapel kids can't believe their eyes. "Is it him? Is it him?"

Michael smiles and says hello. Someone points out the gold domes of a nearby mosque and tells Michael how it was built the wrong way--it doesn't face Mecca.

Michael collapses in mirth.

Copping a Thriller

At around 2:30pm that afternoon Michael visits Marylebone Police Station. His management had contacted the station only that morning to arrange the trip.

"He stayed about an hour and a quarter", Marylebone's Chief Inspector Beer tells "No.1". "He visited our communications center, our cells, the shoplifting unit."

During a break in his guided tour, Michael enjoys a soft drink with Chief Inspector Beer in the senior officers' canteen.

"I just asked him about his plans", says Beer, "and what he thought about the sights of London. He seemed a very ordinary sort of chap."

Michael also inspected a line-up of police officers bearing notebooks and truncheons.

"That was misreported in the press", says Chief Inspector Beer. "It was not a guard of honor--it was just an ordinary parade for the late turn shift. Michael seemed impressed anyway."

At a small reception at CBS Records during the evening Michael is presented with a disc to celebrate the "Thriller" album going platinum. Eight times over...
