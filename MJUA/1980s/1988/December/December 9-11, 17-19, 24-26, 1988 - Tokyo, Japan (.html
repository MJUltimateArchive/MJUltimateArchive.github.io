Tokyo, Japan (Bad World Tour)

 

December 11, 1988 Concert:

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

11th December, 1988 saw 9 year old Ayana Takada become the four millionth person to attend Michael’s Bad Tour. Ayana was brought on stage to collect a special certificate from Michael at the Tokyo Dome, Japan. Michael met both her parents and brother before the show.

Over 855,000 people in Japan saw the show, the most ever attended for that country.

Proceeds from LA’s Sports Arena concert on 27th January 1989 was donated to Childhelp USA, the largest child abuse prevention charity in the States. The “Michael Jackson International Institute for Research On Child Abuse” was founded by Childhelp of Southern California in appreciation of Michael’s efforts.

Michael would never tour the USA again after 27th January, 1989 date at the Memorial Sports Arena.

Around 4.4 million people attended the tour’s 123 concerts, which grossed $125 – easily a world record at the time.

Nominated, at the Inaugural International Rock awards in April 1989, for Tour of the Year 1988 – award went to the human rights organisation Amnesty International’s multi-artist tour (supported by Tracey Chapman, Peter Gabriel, Bruce Springsteen and Sting).
