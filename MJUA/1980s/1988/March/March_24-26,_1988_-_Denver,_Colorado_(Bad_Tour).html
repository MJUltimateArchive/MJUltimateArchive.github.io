Denver, Colorado (Bad Tour)

 

David Nordahl, Painter

Quick notes from Audio Interview (Will Update Soon):

Michael says to come to Denver, Please bring paints and easels to Denver. Got to hotel, waited an hour, Mike's so excited, he couldn't sleep last night, did some drawing, visited 3 or 4 galleries, bookstores, was there all 3 days Michael was in town, Michael accidentally went in large bookstore, got lots of people coming over (“like water to a drainpipe”), went to a small gallery, seller tried to sell him everything, there was a beauty shop across the mall, women hanging on the window, shampoo and hair wraps in head, Told David how he used jackets and sweaters in case he got grabbed, and using costumes to disguise.

David asked him, "Do you work out?", and he said, "No, I should, shouldn't I?", he sounded really guilty about it. David replied, "Hey, whatever you're doing, it's working"

He noticed the vitiligo on the right side of his face, down his neck, and the back of his hand.

Michael visited Make-a-wish foundation kids before Denver shows. Boy died one night in Denver. David asks him, "How can you spend the time with these children that are dying and then go, from there, on stage and give that kind of performance?"

Michael replied,  "How could I not? If these children want to see me. I know *I'm* not important, but Michael Jackson the superstar is. If I can make a child live an extra minute,  or an extra hour, or a day or a month, wouldn't that be worth it?"

“ABC News” (August 23, 2009) (archived)

Nordahl's Jackson period began after the singer invited him to the Denver stop of the Bad tour in March 1988.

"I didn't know what to expect," Nordahl says. "He was sweet. We went to galleries, bookstores and a private showing of the King Tut exhibit. We sat around and laughed and talked and drew."
