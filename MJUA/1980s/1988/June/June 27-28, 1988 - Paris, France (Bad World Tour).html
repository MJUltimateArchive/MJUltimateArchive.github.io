Paris, France (Bad World Tour)

 

Bob Jones, publicist, "Black Radio Exclusive" magazine (July 22, 1988)

Paris—We arrived in the French capital on June 22, and enjoyed a few days of relaxation, shopping and sightseeing. We had a complete tour of the art treasures of the Louvre, including the fabled Mona Lisa. A crowd of at least 500 fans eager to get a glimpse of Michael stood outside our hotel constantly. He made a bit of history here, by becoming the first performer to play the Parc Des Princes (the site of the Pope's visits to Paris). There were 65,000 in attendance the first night and 69,000 the second! Among them were Albertine Bongo, daughter of the President of Gabon, a former French African colony. By the way, her mother, Josephine, owns Hollywood-based Elumba Recording Studios. Jet-setters were treated to a swinging after-party hosted by international Disco queen Regine, who attended the first show with a huge party and came back as our guest the following night.
