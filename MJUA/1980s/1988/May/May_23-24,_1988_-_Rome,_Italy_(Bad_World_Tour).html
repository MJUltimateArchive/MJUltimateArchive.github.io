Rome, Italy (Bad World Tour)

 

Bob Jones, publicist, "Black Radio Exclusive" magazine (July 22, 1988)

Michael's three sold-out shows at the Stadio Flaminio attracted 45,000 each night. After the first show, CBS Records International hosted a sumptuous sit-down buffet for 500 guests. Leaving Rome, we drove to Florence, where Michael enjoyed the historic beauty of the city and visited the museum to see Michaelangelo's masterpiece sculpture of "David".

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

Michael had a request for a private audience with Pope John Paul turned down while visiting Italy for the opening night of the European leg of his tour. Unfortunately the Pope had a backlog of applications to meet bishops, who were a priority. A spokesperson said:

“Usually he is very open to requests from artistes. But we’ve had a backlog of requests building up while the Pope was away in South America. We can’t count them all.”

Michael turned up at film director Franco Zeffirelli’s villa in Rome during his stay in Italy where he also met film stars Sophia Loren and Gina Lollobrigida.

Michael made a visit to the Sistine Chapel, where he was said to be overwhelmed at being inside viewing the famous works of Michelangelo and other renowned artists.

A few hours before opening night in Rome Michael paid a visit to the Bambino Gesu Hospital Children’s Hospital.

Later he slipped out the Bryon Hotel alone in disguise to go and see some of the sights of Rome. Security panicked when they discovered he was missing but put at ease when he later returned walking past his fans unrecognised.
