"Barnes & Noble" Book Store Visit

 

“Daily Express” (July 10, 2008)

[Michael] visited a Las Vegas book store in a wheelchair and wearing slippers and pyjama bottoms.

Only the almost obligatory surgical mask and red baseball cap gave clues that this was… 47-year-old Jackson hiding from the public in his latest disguise.

Sadly, the outfit only served to draw more attention to the superstar, who used bodyguards to push him all the way to the door before abandoning the chair and shopping on foot inside the store.

His children came with him, also sporting baseball caps.

But Prince Michael Jackson Jr, 11, Paris Jackson, 10, and Prince Michael Jackson II, eight, did not seem to mind the waiting paparazzi as much as their father.

The family left the shop with armfuls of comic books, and Jacko again chose the wheelchair for the journey home.

The singer is currently living at Las Vegas’ Hilton hotel while he works on a new album.

 

Unknown newspaper snippet

His features are obscured by a mask, scarf, and dark glasses.

But there are no prizes for guessing the identity of this... character being pushed in a wheelchair.

It is, of course, Michael Jackson, who adopted his latest, not-so-cunning disguise for a visit to a toy and book shop near his current home in Las Vegas.

Accompanying him were his children, Prince Michael, aged 11, Paris, ten, and six0year-old Prince Michael II (also known as Blanket). They, unusually, were not wearing masks.

Completing the party was an eight-strong security team, including the minder responsible for pushing Jackson's chair.

Once inside the Barnes & Noble store, the singer, who turns 50 next month, dispensed with his helper and wheeled himself straight to the children's book section, where he spent 30 minutes before heading to the music section. An onlooker said: "There were quite a few members of the public in the shop, but no one approached him.

"To be honest, he looked a bit weird wheeling himself around covered up in a surgeon's mask. He was in the shop about two hours in total. WHen they left, the children came out first and he was wheeled out behind them."

...A family friend indicated last night that [the wheelchair] was indeed needed because of a recurring back problem Jackson has had since his performing days.

The star is currently trying to land a residency at one of the big venues in Las Vegas to clear massive debts.

 

“Daily Mirror” (July 10, 2008)

Michael Jackson is set to make a comeback with golden oldie boyband New Kids On The Block.

The King of Pop had secret talks about the link-up with the band's stars at his home in Nevada last week.

New Kids singer Donny Wahlberg, 38, said: "It's very exciting. It's one big party and everyone wants to join us." The pop veterans announced the plan as they celebrated the launch of their first reunion tour in 20 years which starts in September.

New Kids were the original US boyband and sold 80 million records with hits including Hanging Tough and Right Stuff.

And they are just some of the artists collaborating with Jacko, 49, as he attempts to revive his career following his acquittal three years ago of child abuse charges.

The Bad singer was due to release his much anticipated album this year to coincide with his 50th birthday.

But a source said: "He has been in the studio for months. He wants to create an amazing album and won't release anything until he is totally satisfied.

"He's a musical genius and record bosses want to leave him to develop his magic. It will be down to him when it is released."
