“Daily Star” (November 14, 2008)

He has been forced to hand over the ownership deeds after legal papers were filed in Santa Barbara, California.

The reclusive 50-year-old singer turned the 2,500-acre property into a fairytale theme park to entertain children.

But Jacko’s shrinking income failed to match his lavish spending and he hit financial troubles that nearly cost him the ranch earlier this year.

Los Angeles company Colony Capital stepped in at the last minute to buy the £17million mortgage the Thriller singer could not pay, saving the property from going under an auctioneer’s hammer.

Jacko was in effect getting a refinancing loan but now, according to the legal papers, Jackson has handed over the deeds to Sycamore Valley Ranch Co, LLC.

Neverland was called Sycamore Ranch before Jackson bought it in 1998, and the new owner is a corporation that is a joint venture between him and Colony Capital.

With Jackson no longer having control of Neverland’s destiny, it could now be sold off.

The star is currently in Las Vegas working on a comeback album.

He recently shot down reports that he was planning a Jackson 5 reunion.
