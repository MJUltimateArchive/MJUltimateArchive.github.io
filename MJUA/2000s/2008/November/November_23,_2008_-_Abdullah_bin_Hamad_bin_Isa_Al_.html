Abdullah bin Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa vs Michael Jackson (Settlement Date)

 

“Daily Star” (November 21, 2008)

The singer, 50, has agreed to come here in person to testify after originally asking to appear via videolink.

He is being sued by Sheikh Abdulla bin Hamad Al Khalifa.

The King of Bahrain’s second son claims he gave the singer £4.7million for a joint music project.

He took Jackson under his wing in 2005 after the star was cleared of child abuse charges in the US, London’s High Court heard yesterday.

He showered him with gifts and hospitality in return for an album, a candid autobiography and a stage play.

But Jackson left the country without sticking to his half of the deal, claims Sheikh Al Khalifa. Jackson’s team insist the money was a “gift”. He will give evidence on Monday.

The Sheikh, 33, said he no longer wanted a music deal, simply his money back. He told the court: “To me it was never seen as a gift.”

The court also heard the Sheikh paid Jackson’s £1.4m US trial costs.

Jackson claims there was no valid agreement because he never read it and was emotionally exhausted.

The hearing continues.
