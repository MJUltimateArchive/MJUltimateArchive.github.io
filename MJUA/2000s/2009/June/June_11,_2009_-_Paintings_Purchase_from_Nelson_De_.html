Paintings Purchase from Nelson De La Nuez

Date confirmed in “TMZ” (“June 11 [2009]”)

 

“TMZ” (June 29, 2009) (archived)

On June 11, Jackson purchased a picture of the Wicked Witch of the West, a painting called "The Donut Queen," and a painting called "Play Ball." The artist for all three works was Nelson De La Nuez. Jackson plunked down $13,000 for the three items, which were delivered to his home on June 15.

We're told Jackson was "obsessed" with the Wicked Witch painting.

Here's the weirdest part. Someone from AEG handled the money side of the transaction. We have the bank wire -- AEG dealt directly with the art seller.

 

Nelson De La Nuez, personal “Facebook” page (June 27, 2009) (archived)

Mr. Jackson recently purchased several pieces of art from me. I had the honor of meeting him and delivering the artwork to his home shortly before his death. He was a childhood idol of mine and an entertainer who broke ground and set new standards for those to come. He was in a league of his own though and really, in a world of his own. He was mysterious and talented and the world constantly wanted to know who the man behind the mask was. I can tell you..he was a kind, gentle, soft-spoken man who did me the honor of loving my art and I hope my creativity brought him joy. I am deeply saddened and shocked by the timing and irony of the recent events which have taken place. My life has been changed forever. You can't meet someone like that... an enigma, a piece of history, an idol and not be changed. The saddest part is that Michael Jackson had just started a "relationship" with my art and myself and who knows what would have come of it? Now we'll never know, but I consider myself lucky for having the brief moments I had to meet a legend and place my paintings on his walls.
