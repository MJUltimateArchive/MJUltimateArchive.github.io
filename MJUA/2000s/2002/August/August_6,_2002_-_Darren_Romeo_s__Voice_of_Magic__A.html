"Siegfried & Roy" Attendance

Date confirmed in “Q” magazine (“6 August 2002”)

 

“Q” magazine

The animal-loving star dropped into Las Vegas' famous Mirage casino to watch a performance by magician Darren Romeo, a protege of flamboyant illusionists Siegfried & Roy. "This is what I always wanted to capture in my shows", said Jackson [after seeing Romeo’s spellbinding show]. "Music and magic is a fantastic combination; you two and Darren may have to develop some magic for my songs when I stage my next concert."

A keen amateur magician, Jackson had spent the afternoon shopping for new tricks in Vegas magic shops. "He was interested in coin tricks", magic store manager Scott Alexander told the press.

...Following Romeo's show, Fischbacher whisked Jackson backstage to meet the latest addition to their menagerie. "He had met them before, but he had never bottle-fed one", recalls Romeo.

...Jackson spent three minutes feeding the animal with a mixture of milk and protein. "He wasn't afraid at all", says Romeo. "He's a very spiritual person and he loves animals. He was completely fascinated by its markings."

 

"Jet" magazine (August 26, 2002)

Superstar musician Michael Jackson shares a laugh with his longtime friends, acclaimed Las Vegas magicians Siegfried & Roy, as he feeds the duos' rare snow-white male Siberian White tiger, Apollo. Jackson, who wrote Siegfried & Roy's theme song, was in Las Vegas to see their protégé, the "Voice of Magic" Darren Romeo, at The Mirage in Las Vegas. "This is what I always wanted to capture in my shows," said Jackson after seeing Romeo's spellbinding show, which combines magic and music. "Music and magic is a fantastic combination; the three of you may have to develop some magic for my songs when I stage my next concert."
