Munich, Germany (The Jackson 5 European Tour):

“Soul” newspaper (January 18, 1973)

Though [The Jackson Five] didn't like German food, they loved rambling through the Olympic Village in Munich and noted where the Israeli athletes were held as hostages. The Jackson Five and Randy were each given two solid gold coins and souvenirs from the German government. They all toured a German brewery--but none got a chance to sample the famous beer.

...Customs inspections was somewhat of an inconvenience, they said. In Munich, the airport officials thoroughly searched each passenger on the J-5 jet. Jermaine was sent through the metal searching device several times until his keys were discovered as the source of distraction.

"We would wait until we got to the airport to get things duty-free", Marlon explained.

"But when we went through customs in the States, we ended up paying the tax anyway!"

 

"Scene and Heard" Radio Interview:

“Scene and Heard” interview (November 12, 1972), from Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro” (article summary/quotes)

One of Michael’s rarest interviews from days gone by was one with Nicky Horne for the Radio 1 programme Scene & Heard.

While touring for the first time in Britain in November, 1972 Michael spoke to Horne about how they were discovered, on rehearsals after school and the Jackson 5 TV show.

Michael stuck with the old trusted story of how Diana Ross discovered them at a show and spoke to them backstage. Most days were spent rehearsing for their upcoming shows after school. Michael said it was safe to go out down to the stores where he lived as most people knew the family and didn’t both them. From time to time friends would come over to play either basketball or swim. He was currently learning to play the piano at the time of the interview.

Michael said he liked the slow songs best as you could understand the lyrics better.
