Atlanta, Georgia (The Jackson 5 US Tour)

 

“Jet” Magazine (February 3, 1972)

Headlining the first annual Martin Luther King Jr. Birthday Commemoration Concert at Atlanta’s Metropolitan Auditorium, The Jackson 5, of Motown, performed for 5,000 wildly cheering persons while more than 3,000 stood helplessly outside. Proceeds from the concert--847,600--were turned over to Mrs. Coretta Scott King, the slain martyr’s widow (for the MLK Center for Social Change) by Black promoter Henry Wynn, who contributed his professional services. Appearing with The Jackson 5 were Atlanta-born and reared Gladys Knight and The Pips and comedienne Jackie (Moms) Mabley. Rep. Ronald Dellums (D., Calif.), Mrs. King and the Rev. Andrew Young addressed the audience.
