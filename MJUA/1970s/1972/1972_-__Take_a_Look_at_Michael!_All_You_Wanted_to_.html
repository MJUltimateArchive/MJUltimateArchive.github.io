Date range confirmed in Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

 

"Take a Look at Michael! All You Wanted to Know" article (1972), from Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro” (article summary / quotes)

Despite Michael’s success with his brothers, he was still expected to do his share of the chores around the house. All instilled by his parents to keep their feet firmly on the ground.

When they do have spare time, Michael pointed out they have everything they need.

“We don’t have to go out of the gate much. You see everything is here.”

On returning to the old Jackson Street house:

“I’m really surprised when we go back there now. Imagine there were only two rooms for the whole family to live in.”

On his father’s belief they would make it:

“He started us off, and kept us going. When we’d come home from school, he’d make us rehearse, and kept saying to us, ‘work on it boys. One day you’ll go to Hollywood.’ I never really believed him then, but it turned out, he was right.”

Has he ever been frightened on stage?

“Once in Detroit, the kids rushed on stage, the barricades came down, and some of them were hurt.”
