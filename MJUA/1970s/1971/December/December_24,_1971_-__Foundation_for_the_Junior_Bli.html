"Foundation for the Junior Blind" Concert

Date confirmed in “Baltimore Afro-American” (archived) (“last Friday [December 24, 1971]”)

 

“Baltimore Afro-American” (January 1, 1972) (archived)

More 400 blind children from the Foundation for the Junior Blind were as enthusiastic as any sighted audience in their response to the Jackson 5 at a Christmas concert given by the Motown superstars last Friday.

Questioned at the end of the concert about his response to the evening, Michael Jackson said quietly, “You know, really, this is what Christmas is all about—giving.”

The J-5 Christmas gift to the blind children was a full one, as all of the Jackson family was on hand for the performance. Eight- year-old Randy Jackson, playing conga drums, joined his brothers for the. first time, in the 45-minute show which Jackie Jackson described as "one of the most rewarding" performances we have ever given.”

The blind children had learned the lyrics to the string of performed J-5 hits, and were prepared for the show, which for the most part, turned into a sing-a-long. Their enthusiasm differed in no way from that of any other J-5 audience.

Perhaps the most mutually rewarding part of the evening came at the end of the performance when, for the first time since the J-5 made their overwhelming debut into the world of show business, almost three years ago, the group was able to chat and shake hands with youngsters in the audience.

The Christmas concert was the first of two appearances that the J-5 will make for the underprivileged children this holiday season. The Motown recording artists, dressed as Santa Clauses, will distribute Christmas presents at a Motown Christmas party on Wednesday.
