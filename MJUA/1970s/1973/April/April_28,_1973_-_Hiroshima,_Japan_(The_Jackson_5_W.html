Hiroshima, Japan (The Jackson 5 World Tour)

 

"Soul" Newspaper (June 25, 1973)

In Hiroshima, there were quite a few Black servicemen from the military base only forty miles away. As luck would have it, this was the one time during the entire tour that something went wrong with the sound. A fuse blew, knocking out the mikes and with the language barrier, it was taking a little longer than usual to fix.

Undaunted, Tito broke into a blues-jazz riff on his guitar (still working directly through the amp and tore the house down. While he cooked in the style of B.B. King and T. Bone Walker. Brothers bounced into the aisles solo and with each other doing the latest U.S. steps and hollering "cook, get down and preach Brother" in gratitude for a touch of some down home soul.
