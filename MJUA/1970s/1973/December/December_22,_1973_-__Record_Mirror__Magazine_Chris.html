"Record Mirror" Magazine Christmas Message

Date of publication confirmed in Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro” (article summary/quotes)

Michael sent this good will message and thoughts on Christmas via Robin Katz who he spoke with from Los Angeles for her regular Jackson 5 column in Record Mirror.

“On behalf of all of us in the Jackson Five, Merry Christmas. We’ve been very lucky this year and I think that’s about the best anybody could get for Christmas…good luck, and being very healthy. Presents are very good too, but being happy is much more important. We’re sorry that we didn’t make it to England this year. I hope you don’t think that we’ve forgotten you, because it’s not like that at all. We had other things we had to do…and one of them was school.”

Michael continued by saying how they celebrated Christmas by having the family over and singing around the piano. Jermaine’s girlfriend Hazel’s family were invited this particular year, Berry Gordy was her father. There was also one more addition, Tito’s new son Taj.

The brothers would also be visiting some local hospitals which was something Michael loved to do.

“We don’t usually see who’s in our audiences, but when you go into a hospital ward you see everyone. It’s the greatest feeling, you know. And you don’t have to be in a group to go around cheering up less fortunate people at Christmas.”

 

Robin Katz, “Record Mirror” editor, “Record Mirror” (November 25, 1972) (mirror)

There were several times on the tour when Jermaine and Michael, in particular, would team up to do some very funny things so that I would have something to write about.

There was one meal that I was eating, and Michael came up behind me very quietly and just said, “How come whenever I see you, you always have something in your mouth?

I turned very red till I realised he was just pulling my leg.
