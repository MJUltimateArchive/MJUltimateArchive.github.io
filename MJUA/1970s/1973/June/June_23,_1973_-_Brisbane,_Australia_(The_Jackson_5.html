Brisbane, Australia (The Jackson 5 World Tour)

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

As the show in Brisbane closed with ‘I Wanna Be Where You Are’ the crowd broke through the security cord, leaving the Jacksons to fight through the screaming girls to their limousine.

 

Velda Mazoudier, date, “Northern Territory News” (June 27, 2009)

Velda Ma­zoudier told the North­ern Ter­ri­tory News last night that Jack­son had been her first boyfriend and they had stayed in touch for al­most a decade af­ter meet­ing in 1973.

‘‘I was 15 and I was love struck,’’ she said.

‘‘I re­mem­ber kiss­ing him and that was prob­a­bly my first kiss. My mother was hor­ri­fied. I don’t know if he would have in­ter­preted it that way, but I did.’’

But she said she was un­sure if she was Jack­son’s first love.

‘‘It would be a lovely thought and I think I would prob­a­bly be dream­ing think­ing about that, but I don’t know,’’ she said. ‘‘He was only 15.’’ Ms Ma­zoudier, 50, of Katherine, said she met Jack­son in Bris­bane in July 1973 when he was tour­ing Aus­tralia with the Jack­son 5.

She was shop­ping with her mother in the Queen St Mall when the Jack­sons’ limou­sine pulled up.

Ms Ma­zoudier said that she and her mother went up to ask for Jack­son’s au­to­graph, and he responded by ask­ing her out.

‘‘I was only about 15... we went out that night but my mom had to come,’’ she said.

They ended their date with a kiss and for the next eight years Ms. Ma­zoudier and Jack­son ex­changed let­ters as he rose from a boy won­der to the big­gest pop star on the planet.

‘‘He used to write the most won­der­ful let­ters,’’ she said.

‘‘Ev­ery song I ever lis­tened to I imag­ined he was writ­ing it for me.’’

Ms Ma­zoudier said the let­ters dried up in the early 1980s when she got mar­ried and he be­came a megas­tar.

She said the last thing she re­ceived from Jack­son was a signed copy of his 1983 smash hit sin­gle Beat It.

‘‘I tried to con­tact him many times af­ter that, but I never heard back from him,’’ she said.
