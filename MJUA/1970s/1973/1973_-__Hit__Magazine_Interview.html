"Hit" Magazine Interview

Date confirmed in Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

 

“Hit” magazine (1973), from Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro” (article summary/quotes)

Michael discusses his daydreams with Laura Smart in this interview in 1973.

“I’m really lucky in as much as my career is going well at such an early age. I mean I don’t even pretend to think that I’m grown up and that there’s anything great I can do to help people.”

Michael does admit to being selfish sometimes and wonders how long it going to take the fan club secretary to answer all his mail. He also admits to day dreaming a lot.

“I don’t often discuss my dreams with people because then I think they’ll never come true if I tell anyone. So I’d rather not say what the big ones are.”

Michael said when the group first started out he used to day dream all the time about making hit records and going to England to play concerts.

Despite gaining fame Michael still remembered people’s birthdays and special events.

“Some of my friends, outside the music business, really envy me because they say I have such a happy home life. I used to take it for granted, but I’ve been to some of my friends’ homes and realised things are not so good. I can’t imagine not being happy.”

Speaking about boredom Michael said:

“When I’m on my own I never feel bored. I go fishing o read or draw or just kick a ball around in the back yard. But it’s usually when I’m on my own that I start to drift off into another world and imagine myself, say ten years from now. It’s quite frightening to think that far ahead, but I wish I knew what the situation was going to be then!”
