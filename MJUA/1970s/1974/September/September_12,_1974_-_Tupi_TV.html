Carlos A. Gouveau, Folha De S. Paulo (September 13, 1974) (Image 003) (translated)

The Jackson Five and its entourage composed of 21 people landed yesterday at the airport of Viracopos at the scheduled time, 1:30 pm. However, they only managed to get to the Othon Palace Hotel, where they are staying, around 6pm. According to his businessmen, Koski and Ellis, the delay was due to the existing bureaucracy in Viracopos and also the determination of the group's chief of security that imposes the musicians that go together.

Quite apart from Alice Cooper's press conference, many strangers to the press were present, everything went smoothly with the Jackson Five. The members of the group were tired and asked that questions be asked quickly.

The first question inevitably was about Brazil, and the answer, of course, was flattering. "A true Mafia involves the phonographic world, with its most intense activities in the United States and Europe." When that question was formulated, the six elements of the Jackson Five looked at their father and businessman Joe Jackson, who simply replied that "they do not answer that kind of question."

Drinking soft drinks, the guys in the set were answering the most difficult questions for the father. Two questions slightly embarrassed old, callous Joe Jackson. The first was about the inclusion of Randy, the younger brother of the group, at age 12. The reporter referred to the change of voice of Michael Jackson, which until here was the withdrawal of the group, which now needed another child to continue to be a commercial attraction. Joe Jackson, after a brief hesitation, said that "Randy came in because he wanted to come in", which did not convince anyone.

The other question was whether the fact that the parents accompany the group everywhere would be more of a commercial motivation to create a positive image for the whole. Joe once again scratched his head and said no, because he travels under the condition of an entrepreneur.

Although they follow the soul-music line and sing for a certain type of audience. Jermaine Jackson said that they play soul and rock, and that their audience is of all ages. Quite the difference between art and commerce, Michael Jackson was intruded on his father when he tried to respond. Joe said that everyone was satisfied with the productions that have been made so far, and that the solo albums of Michael and Jermaine do not represent a divergence of style, but new works put up for sale.
