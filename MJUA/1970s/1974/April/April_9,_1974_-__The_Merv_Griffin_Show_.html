"The Merv Griffin Show"

Filming date confirmed with the air date of April 10, 1974 being confirmed on April 10, 1974 newspaper TV listing (archived) (“WEDNESDAY, APRIL 10, 1974”); talk shows are typically filmed the day before.

 

“The Merv Griffin Show” interview transcript

Merv Griffin: You big guys are not going to tell me you pick your own costumes? You must have a costume designer or something?

Jackie: Yes, we have a lady by the name of Ruthie West, who designed all of our costumes.

Griffin: Don't let her go. Did she look at you and say "Mm-hm, you're going to be shocking pink. Michael's gonna be blue, Jermaine's gonna be darker blue, and yours [points at Marlon] a purple passion", huh? Who got all their hair pulled out in Europe? I heard about that.

Michael: Ohhh...

Tito: Tell us, Mike.

Griffin: Was it you, Michael?

Michael: Well, it really was all of us. We went to London, the first one we went to, and we arrived at the airport. There were thousands of kids sleeping in packing bags--

Jackie: Sleeping bags.

Michael: They were just waiting for us and, when we arrived, there's a lot of people there and they just--while they were grabbing us, and so, all the guards and the police--they made a long line, so we can walk through, and we walked it all--everybody just caved in on us. I had on a tie, and they were choking me this way. I lost my shoe, and--

Griffin: That'll get the voice higher and higher.

Michael: Yeah.

Griffin: Well, somewhere in England, there's a little lady walking around with handfuls of hair, right? She might be having a lot of money on that, Michael.

Michael: Well, everybody had some hair.

Griffin: Where'd you play in London? Albert Hall?

Michael: No, we played at the Wembley Stadium. And we were the first ones ever to break the record there. We broke the Beatles' record. [Audience applause]

Griffin: Well, I don't doubt it for a minute. I mean it, it was an entertaining group. It really swamps you to see you fellas in performance. And tonight, you open at the MGM Grand Hotel.

Jackie: First time.

Griffin: Not the first time in Las Vegas.

Jackie: First time in Las Vegas.

Griffin: Let me tell you about that town. Now, we better get the gospel songs out. It's a very sinful city. Don't leave your rooms, okay? Oh, you're gonna love it there. It's so nice. After you left London, where'd you go?

Michael: We went to Paris, then we went to Amsterdam, to Germany, then we went to London, then we went over to Australia, then Japan, New Zealand. And our last trip was Africa, was--really was great.

Griffin: Where in Africa did you play?

Michael: Dakar, Senegal.

Griffin: How many turned out?

Michael: How many? 100,000.

Griffin: 100,000 people? At a dollar a head... [Group laughs] You don't need another brother, do you? Jackson 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6... [points to Randy] Well, who are you? You didn't wander in, and just join this crowd, did you?

Randy: No, first time I played on stage was here. We played "Hellzapoppin'", my first television show.

Griffin: Oh, yeah, that was that big show they did on this stage here. They hit each other in the head with pies and chickens.

Randy: From then, I started traveling all over.

Griffin: I mean, you were an immediate hit on that show, and the brothers said, "Let's put him with us." Right? And you play the drums?

Randy: Congas.

Jackie: Oh, Randy, why don't you tell 'em about the oatmeal boxes?

Michael: Oh, when you first started playing congas.

Griffin: You played a tune on your breakfast cereal?

Randy: When they first started singing, they were singing a long time ago, and I didn't have nothing to do, so I just sat up ad watched 'em. So, I got the Quaker Oatmeal boxes--

Griffin: Oh yeah, the round ones.

Randy: Yeah, and I put 'em all together with a stick, and I started beating 'em. So, I waited a while and my father asked me was I ready, so I wasn't ready. And about another year, he said--

Griffin: "Would you like to come and play oatmeal?" How do you get from oatmeal boxes to conga drums though?

Randy: Well, my father bought the set of conga drums, and that's when I started.

Griffin: Are there more at home like you, just waiting in the wings?

Tito: Yeah, we have three sisters. There's nine kids all together. We have a large [family]. Do they sing?

Tito: Well, I'd like to mention the fact that my whole family is going to be doing Vegas. My little sister Janet, she's seven, she's gonna do a solo with Randy. They're gonna be singing together, some of the "Sonny & Cher", great songs. My sister Toya's gonna be tap dancing with us, it's gonna be really great.

Griffin: It's gonna be a regular Mormon Tabernacle Choir. [Audience laughs] I think that's wonderful.

Michael: Don't you do your show in Vegas sometimes?

Griffin: Yep, Caesar's Palace. We're going there in about--we go there the end of April into May.

Michael: Yeah.

Griffin: Yeahhh. They're the greatest audiences for a nightclub. But you're used to a hundred thousand. I mean, this must seem like a combo to you.

Michael: Yeah, we went up there to see Paul Anka's show, and it was really great. It was fantastic.

Michael: He's a really nice guy.

Griffin: I can't tell which--well, except for Michael and Randy, I can't tell--you all look the same age. Who's the old man?

Jackie: I am.

Griffin: Well, one is married. Well, that's Jermaine--[points to Jackie] Are you married? [Jackie nods] Well, then Jermaine--I know Jermaine's married. He married the boss's daughter, do-doo-do-do [snaps fingers]... You marry Berry Gordy's daughter... Watch this, say it three times fast. Berry Gordy's--

Michael: Daughter.

Griffin: Yeah, but say Berry Gordy's daughter. Say it three times fast.

Michael: Berry Gordy's daughter. Berry Gordy's--oh, I can't. [Audience laughs]

Griffin: Don't laugh at my mistakes [laughs] Then one other is married, is it you Randy? No? Tito: I'm married.

Griffin: Are you married?

Tito: Yes.

Griffin: Are there children yet?

Tito: Yeah, I have one son, and I've been married two years. Now, I have a six-month-old son.

Griffin: You made this crowd a bunch of uncles?

Tito: Yeah. Well, I have a sister who's 24 and we've been uncles three years now.

Griffin: [Laughs] Are you more protected here with security than you are--well, I suppose in Europe, you're more particular, aren't you?

Michael: Oh, yes, a lot is--

Griffin: Do the police travel with you?

Jackie: Sure.

Michael: Yeah. We have security on the floors. Girls always sneak up, they all come to the elevator, 'cause they know there are polices waiting on them, so they go through the stairs. [Michael absently itches his leg]

Griffin: Makes him itch. But it's nice--well, maybe one or two break through, and you get to shake hands.

Jermaine: Oh, well, after the show, we all come upstairs and have a little party, and sign autographs.

Michael: But not all of them.

Jackie: We go to bed every night eleven o'clock.

Griffin: Do you?

Marlon: We have to be in bed at eleven.

Jackie: 11:30.

Griffin: Even the married guys?

Jackie: Everybody.

Marlon: Because we do one-nighters, and we have to get up sometimes at eight, sometimes the plane leaves and we have to leave the hotel at seven, so--then we have to get rest before the show, so have to get up early.

Griffin: Is it ever though that the work seems like it's too much?

Marlon: Well, if you like it, it's not too much. We really like it.

Griffin: How old are you now, Michael?

Michael: Fourteen.

Griffin: So you've grown up right in front of us. Are you gonna keep getting taller and taller?

Michael: I don't know [audience laughs]

Marlon: The first time we did our show was here too. We did the Diana Ross Special here.

Griffin: It was Diana Ross who discovered you, wasn't it?

Group: Right, sure yeah.

Griffin: A mayor brought you to her.

Jackie: No, we all started in Gary, Indiana.

Griffin: Wasn't it the mayor who brought you?

Jackie: Yeah, we were playing for a benefit show in Gary, Indiana and Diana Ross was the mayor's guest. And we entertained for the mayor, the showing, and after we entertained, she came backstage and said we were very, very, good. So, just have to take us to Motown and entertain for Berry Gordy and executive staff there. After that, our first record we put out was a two million seller, and after that everything started going well for us.

Griffin: I don't know Berry Gordy, but he really must be a genius, isn't he?

Jackie: He's a very nice man.

Griffin: I mean, look at the superstars he's made, and he knows how to publicize an act, and he knows how to make stars out of people. It's extraordinary.

Michael: Really smart.

Griffin: Do you sit around and decide what you're going to sing, or is it all done for you?

Jackie: Well, we all get together, us and our producers, and Miss--in fact, we have a 24-track recording studio at home, and this will--

Griffin: 24-track?

Jackie: Yeah, we do all our stuff at home now, at our house.

Griffin: [Inaudible] [Jackie and Michael laugh] I think I want to do a commercial, 'cause this audience is--they're all breathing together, they're so excited about hearing you sing again, and I am too. We'll be back after this, we're in the Jackson 5--[tape cuts]
