Lake Tahoe, Sierra Nevada (The Jackson 5 World Tour)

Interview date confirmed in “Disc” magazine (“I was talking to the Jacksons...--they were in Lake Tahoe doing another cabaret engagement”)

 

"Disc" Magazine (June 8, 1974) (archived)

One of the most lovable and loved show-business families are due back in town next week, after an absence of two years. Last time they were here they set the whole place ablaze with admiration for their style, talent, and professionalism. Even the heaviest of pop pundits were to be heard singing their praises...

No prizes for guessing we're talking about The Jackson Five--who ought by rights to be called at least The Jackson Six, if not Eight. Why?

Well, they've just embarked upon a highly successful cabaret career in the States, and on stage they not only have little brother Randy, but also sisters Latoya and Janet. So why haven't they rechristened the group the Jackson Six?

"That sounds kind of silly", Jackie told me. "Anyway, we wouldn't call it 'The Jackson Six'--Randy's so small, it would have to be 'The Jackson Five and a Half'!

"We've become known as 'The Jackson Five', and however many of us there were, I think we'd stick to that name. In fact, on stage, there are a lot more, although we only have eight people in the family. We're backed by a 40 piece orchestra!"

That is only for the cabaret shows though, we're not likely to get the Jackson 48 here, just six. This new found success started for the Jacksons at Easter. And as Michael said, they're all pretty happy about it.

"We did a couple of weeks at a hotel in Las Vegas and broke all the attendance records. We didn't know what it would be like, but it was really nice and the audience was so different to the ones we usually get."

Or as Jackie had elaborated:

"We could actually hear what we were singing!" He went on to explain to me what changes had been made to the group's act for the cabaret circuit:

"Because we've got some older people in the audience, we have to do a few older songs as well as our hits. So we're doing things like the Coasters' Yakety Yak and material from people like the Andrews Sisters, the Four Freshmen, and the Mills Brothers."

When they arrive in Britain next week we won't be treated to any of those "golden oldies".

"Oh no, we'll be doing only concerts. But the fans will notice a difference in the show. We do shorter versions of our hits and there is some new material in the show--tracks from our albums, like 'Get It Together'".

We'll also have the opportunity of seeing the boys do "Dancing Machine"--their last number one single in the States, which is being released here (in those immortal words) to coincide with the tour.

I was talking to the Jacksons courtesy of the wonders of transatlantic communication telecommunications--they were in Lake Tahoe doing another cabaret engagement.

But even across those thousands of miles you could hear that they were all excited to be coming back to Britain:

"It'll be really nice--we'll be happy to see all our English fans again, and we hope to get out a bit more this time. We want to go sightseeing and buy some clothes." That was Michael's reaction.

"I haven't bought any suits since the last time we were in Britain", said Jackie. "They're so much nicer than the ones we can get in the States--and they fit much better too. So I'll  be buying a few more suits and some other clothes too. We hope to go down to the Kings Road if we've got the time."

Tito will be bringing his wife Dolores with him again, and he hopes to have a chance to take her round to see the sights this time. After all, on the last visit Tito and his brothers were almost confined to their hotel--by their fans who had gathered outside, and the Osmonds fans who'd gathered to see their idols. They were staying in the same hotel!

Randy will also be with them, and instead of a guest appearance he now does the complete show with his brothers. So it'll be a whole family outing.

The Jacksons are hoping fervently that there'll be no recurrence of the recent crowd troubles at their concerts. They were very sad when I told them about the death of the Cassidy fan.

"it's very unfortunate. We have had some trouble at our concerts, and girls often faint", said Tito. "But if anything serious happened, then we'd stop the show". Let's hope that won't be necessary.

That was all they had time to tell me, although there was a lot more to say. You See, Michael had to get to school. But before they went they sent the message:

"Send our love to all our fans, and tell them we'll see them real soon."
