Queen's Silver Jubilee / "Look-in" Magazine Interview

Date assessed by the fact that Michael said they performed “in Scotland”, and the date the Queen appeared in Scotland on the Silver Jubilee schedule was “May 17, 1977” (archived).

 

Michael Jackson, unknown date (Michael Jackson, In His Own Words)

We got to meet the Queen and she seemed to be genuinely interested in us and asked us where we came from and how we got started. The Duke Of Edinburgh asked us whether our family was in show business before us and whether we played instruments. It was really nice.

Lisa Robinson Interview, (June 9, 1977)

We just came back from Europe and we performed for the Queen of England in Scotland. We’d done it five years ago for her mother, but this time it was for her and her husband, the Duke of Edinburgh. They asked us and we were honored to do it. Afterwards she came backstage and she said, “Did you just come here to perform for [me]?” And we said, “Yes.” She said, “Where do you go next?” We said, “London.” She said, “Are you all brothers?” And we said, “Yes.” And she said our show was very enjoyable. Her husband was very interested—he must have spent five minutes asking us if our parents were musically inclined: what did they play, what did my mother play? My mother played the clarinet in a band, and my father was in a singing group called the Falcons—they were a local group. The Queen had her crown on and a pink dress with all these pearls and rubies and diamonds all over it. She wears a lot of jewelry. The producers and the people from the [Silver] Jubilee told us that [the Queen] did something at our show that they never saw her do—she actually clapped to the music and kept time and nodded her head in keeping the time. We were really happy to hear that; that’s really different and I was glad.

“Moonwalk” autobiography (February 1, 1988)

We had three years of hits behind us when we toured Europe that first time, so there was enough to please both the kids who followed our music and the Queen of England, whom we met at a Royal Command Performance. That was very exciting for us. I had seen photographs of other groups, like the Beatles, meeting the Queen after command performances, but I never dreamed we’d get the chance to play for her.

 

“Look-in” magazine (July 23, 1977), From Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro” (article summary/quotes)

Along with the rest of his brothers Michael spoke with Look-In reporter Gerry Fallon when they visited for their appearance in Glasgow before HM Queen Elizabeth II and The Duke of Edinburgh at the Royal Command Performance, as part of the Queen’s Silver Jubilee celebrations in May 1977.

It was their first appearance in the Britain since November, 1972 and they were now known as The Jacksons, having left Jermaine behind at Motown.

The fans however, had not forgotten them as Michael was quick to acknowledge.

“The fans were great to us. It’s as if we’d never been away for five years!”

Fallon met the Jacksons the day after their Hammersmith Odeon show. Jackie explained that the long delay in coming back to the UK had been because they had been booked continuously in Las Vegas and that they were having problems with Motown.

The Jacksons used London as their base while in Europe, where they made TV appearances in Germany, France and Holland.

Tito recalled a story when Randy fell asleep at his bass drum at a show at the Chicago College.

Michael spoke about The Jacksons plans.

“We’ve a tour that goes through July to the beginning of August. Then I start work on ‘The Wiz’ which is a black musical version of ‘The Wizard Of Oz.”

In it I play the part of the scarecrow. ‘The Wiz’ as been a hit Broadway stage show for the last three years. Other people signed so far are Diana Ross and Bill Cosby. We start filming in August and I should be finished by the end of September.

Michael said he and his brothers planned to diverse in to other things besides music.

“Randy has just signed a contract with Walt Disney Films so he’ll also be going before the movie cameras for the first time. And the others are going into producing and songwriting. Even Jackie as a sideline: he designs the costumes we wear on stage.”
