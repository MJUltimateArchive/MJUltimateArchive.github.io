"Do You Know Where Your Children Are"

Date range assessed by the fact that, according to Matt Forger in Damien Shields’ “Xscape Origins”, it was recorded during the Hayvenhurst sessions before the “Bad” Westlake sessions (“‘It was one of the songs that we started for the Bad album’, recalls Forger. ‘At that time, there were just so many songs that Michael brought to us at Hayvenhurst.’”); the Hayvenhurst sessions were from Mid-1985 through 1986; vocals were most likely recorded in the second year (“Over the course of the second half of 1985 and most of 1986, Michael and the B-Team created many demos that would later be brought to Quincy’s attention at the main Westlake sessions.”) (Mike Smallcombe, “Making Michael”)

 

Chris Cadman, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

‘Do You Know Where Your Children Are’ was leaked on YouTube as the posthumous album MICHAEL was about to be launched. Steve Lukather was called in to provide extra guitar licks, but it didn’t make the final selection, this despite being a strong track.
