"Slave to the Rhythm"

 

 

“Billboard” (May 5, 2014) (archived)

From all this material, Reid and longtime Sony A&R man John Doelp had a clear target. They wanted to find songs [for the “Xscape” album], says Reid, that "Michael sang beginning to end multiple times, multiple tracks, because that was the only indication that I could find that spoke to Michael's love to the songs." Reid knew this well — when he and Edmonds recorded the original "Slave to the Rhythm" with Jackson in Los Angeles in 1989, during the Dangerous sessions, Jackson recorded the vocal 24 times.

"And it was not once and fix the bad note," says Reid. "No, he sang the song from top to bottom 24 times without a bathroom break, without a water break, without a 'Give me a moment.' He would sing the song and say, 'OK, give me another track, I can do it better,' and he'd do it again. 'I can nail this. Give me another track,' and he'd do it again. Each time he's getting better and better, but by the time you got to track 13, 14 we got lost, because it all started to sound the same. At that point he'd perfected it, but he just kept going."

 

Chris Cadman, author, “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

The version leaked at the end of 2010 was one finished by Tricky Stewart with brand new instrumentation after he had been called in by the estate of Michael Jackson. It was due to be included on the MICHAEL release but was left off the final track listing.
