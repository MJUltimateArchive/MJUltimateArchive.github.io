"Buttercup" / Unreleased Stevie Wonder Collaboration Album

Date range confirmed in the “I Want You Back! Unreleased Masters” liner notes (archived) (“Recorded in Los Angeles 1973”)

 

“Rock Fever Special” magazine (1984)

Everyone knows Michael's smash with Paul McCartney on "The Girl Is Mine" and "Say Say Say". They're the most famous superstar songwriting team to come since David Bowie and John Lennon got together to write "Fame" some years ago. What many fans don't know, however, is that long before he ever met McCartney, Michael was part of another superstar collaboration--resulting in an album that has never been released.

It all came about back in the old days when Michael hooked up with another child-star, his close friend. Stevie Wonder.

"That album has some incredible stuff on it", Michael remembers. "I don't really know the reason why Motown holds it. t think they're just waiting until later to put it out and do some numbers with it."

In those early days, before Michael had made his mark as a songwriter  with such hits as "Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough" and "Billie Jean", it was Little Stevie who was the songwriting phenomenon.

How the Songs Happen

"The way he comes up with a song is just incredible", Michael marvels.

"A song comes about in millions of ways--it can take half an hour or half a year--but he comes up with songs just like this." (Michael snaps his fingers). "He'll be fooling around, teasing you, and then write a song on the spot. I've seen him do it lots of times, and you're just amazed."

Obviously, the teaming was influential in the songwriting development  of young Michael, who admits, "I would say I've learned more from him than anybody."

One trait the two music makers have in common is a strong sense of perfectionism. "Stevie's never satisfied with anything". Michael points out.

"He'll do a track forever, that's why it takes him four or five years to do an album. I'll never get over him."

"I wish those songs could have been released back then," he sighs. "But they still could be released now and be #1--God--for a long time..."
