Michael Prince, studio engineer, The MJCast, Episode 52 (March 19, 2017)

I remember talking to one of the guys who was much deeper in Rodney' camp than I, and one of the first things he said is he goes, "I can't believe they didn't put 'Xscape' on there". I go--and I hadn't even heard it, I go, "Can I hear it?" and he played it, and I go--because I was a big fan of the band from the UK in the 80's called "Scritti Politti"... but that song reminded me of that great percussion and the kind of vocals, and the interesting thing was--well, I guess Michael didn't think it was the top 15 but, years later, when I was working with Michael, just he and I in 2008--and it was because of the song Xscape--I always wanted to ask him if he was a fan of "Scritti Polliti", and he lit up when I said that, he said he loved them. He was a huge fan of "Scritti Politti", that I was thinking--I didn't say this to him, I'm going, "Well then why didn't he put Xscape on there?" [laughs] Because it just had that kind of great vibe.
