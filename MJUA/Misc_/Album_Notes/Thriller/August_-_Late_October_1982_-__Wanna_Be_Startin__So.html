"Wanna Be Startin' Somethin'"

Date range assessed by the fact that after recording “The Girl is Mine” in April, “In August 1982, Michael, Quincy, Bruce Swedien, the returning songwriter Rod Temperton and the rest of the team gathered at Westlake’s Studio A to begin recording the follow up to Off the Wall.” and “once Michael had heard the material from the other writers, he finally showed Quincy what he had been working on over the previous year (“Billie Jean” and "Wanna Be Startin' Somethin'")” and “the final days [of recording were] in late October” (Mike Smallcombe, “Making Michael”)

 

Michael Jackson, Chris Cadman’s “Michael Jackson the Maestro”

It’s a song which I had written when we were doing OFF THE WALL, but had never given to Quincy for that album.

“TV Guide” (December 4, 1999)

TV Guide: Which songs disappointed you?

Michael: "Wanna Be Startin' Somethin’." Songwriting is a very frustrating art form. You have to get on tape exactly what's playing inside your head. When I hear it up here [points to his head], it's wonderful. I have to transcribe that onto tape.

“The Daily Beast” (July 6, 2009) (archived)

“Hi,” I said, plopping down. “I’m Abby, and I’ve been wanting to ask you this since I was 12 years old. What does Mamma-Say—Mamma Sah—Mamma-Coo-Sa mean?”

He laughed, a high-pitched chirp that sounded like a castrato. “You’re the second person in my entire career to ask me that!” he trilled. “It means, 'I need you, l love you, I want you’ in Swahili. It fit the song, so I added it in."

 

Bill Wolfer, keyboardist, “Invincible” magazine (November 2014) (mirror) (archived mirror)

There are a lot of synthesizers on those songs–textures, atmospheric stuff–we call those sounds ‘pads.’ Quincy called it “ear candy.”... Wanna Be Startin’ Somethin’ [has] synth pads that come and go, more of a string sound this time, reinforcing the basic chords. I was glad to hear The Waters singing with Michael again. Apparently, I wasn’t the only one who thought they sounded good together! (Michael sang background vocals with the Waters on Bill Wolfer’s debut album “Wolf”, for the tracks “Papa Was a Rollin’ Stone” and “So Shy”, Ed.).

 

“Billboard” (May 5, 2014) (archived)

"Wanna Be Startin' Somethin' " began during the Off the Wall sessions but ended up on Thriller.
