Misc. "Victory" Recording Stories

 

"Tuf Mag" magazine No. 8 (1984)

"Victory" took seven months to complete and was recorded in five different studios, including the 24-track Tito installed in his den. "My wife is mad", he says, "because the carpet is in the hall, and the sofas are in the entryway."

 

"Rock & Soul" magazine (August 1984)

...All of the brothers have studios at home and worked individually on basic tracks before presenting them at group meetings.

Marlon prepared for "Victory" with 40 songs he'd written. But even those songs ultimately became group projects. Randy had been working dilgently with a Los Angeles producer/engineer, and one afternoon during Randy's own recording sessions, Michael and Jackie popped by and began singing new parts to the proposed song-in-progress…

 

"Rock and Soul" magazine (September 1984)

Last September, the Jacksons started putting songs together and came up with 20 smash tunes from which only nine will make the album. For Jermaine, the number is significant.

"I'm excited because working together is something I've waited for nine long years”, revealed Jermaine. “We had never really been together and shown what we could do since the Jackson Five days. It (the LP) came out very well. We had that special magic to our harmonies."
