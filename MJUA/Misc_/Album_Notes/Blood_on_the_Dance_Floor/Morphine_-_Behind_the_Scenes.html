Morphine - Behind the Scenes

 

Brad Buxer, Black & White Magazine interview (translation by SegaLoco)

Black & White: You also play almost all instruments on Morphine ...

Brad Buxer: Yes, but unlike Stranger In Moscow Michael knew exactly what he wanted to hear each instrument pure. He sang all the parts, whether the piano in the middle of the song, or those sheets of synth on the chorus. Everything is his. On this song, so I simply carried out his ideas. With two sound engineers, it has even been singing the word "Morphine" on the chorus. It was very fun …

 

Slash, guitarist, “Q” magazine (August 1995)

Q: How does Jackson work?

Slash: With Michael, the initial tracks that I get to play on are completely bare of vocals, usually just some drums and a little bit of bass, so it's hard to even know what key the song's in. So, I just make up a bunch of stuff and they pick what they want. I did one thing, two things a day, and then they'd say all right, we'll call you back tomorrow. They wouldn't call me back for three months. All of a sudden, I'd get this phone call out of the blue--can you come back?--so I'd come back and then they'd say, OK, we'll call you tomorrow. And then three months would go by... I guess I'm on two songs on his new record. I don't know which the songs are, though. I haven't even heard them yet. That's how it is working with Michael. It's a mystery.
