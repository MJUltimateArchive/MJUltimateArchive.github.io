Superfly Sister - Behind the Scenes

 

Bryan Loren, Black or White Magazine interview (April 1998)

That song has always been one of my favorites. As a matter of fact, Michael and I produced it 50-50. Michael wrote the lyrics by himself but I came up with the title. I remember giving Michael a tape with the basic track, from there we wrote the melody together. A few days later, Michael came back with the lyrics to the song, and we basically recorded it right away.

Bryan Loren’s Facebook page (according to Chris Cadman’s MJ The Maestro

I got the title for Superfly Sister from one of my favourite Heatwave songs, Super Soul Sister.

The groove on this track is a little left of centre. But, that's what I wanted to do with Michael. I wanted to get outside of the box. Much of the material we never got to finish was written along the same thread. He wrote the lyric and recorded most of his vocals in one day. He was, as I'm sure most would imagine, very proficient and a quick study in the studio. We worked very well together. And we always had a great time. That huge handclap in the breaks before the chorus?

A sample (with almost no effect on it) of Michael in the bathroom at Record One Studios.”

Myspace blog post

We actually did the finishing work on ‘Superfly Sister’ from ‘Blood On The Dance Floor’ a few years after I’d already done the track, as this was a song that I started writing during the ‘Dangerous’ sessions.
