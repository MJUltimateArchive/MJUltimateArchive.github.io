"Diana Ross Presents The Jackson 5" Undated Recording Stories

 

Freddie Perren, songwriter, “Musician” magazine (July 1984)

I never saw any songs he wrote, but I always knew he could. There was an instrumental hit out, [Paul Mariat’s] "Love is Blue". He came in one day and asked me about this section of the song he found interesting. So I showed it to him on piano. He couldn't play, but he was able to pick it out with one finger on the piano. He was able to learn this part and would sit there picking it out. There is a certain talent that goes with writing a song, and I could see he had it. Motown just didn’t encourage it in performers. At Motown, the producer was king.
