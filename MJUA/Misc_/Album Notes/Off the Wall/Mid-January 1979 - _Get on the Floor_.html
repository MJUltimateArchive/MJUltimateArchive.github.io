"Get on the Floor"

Date confirmed by Gina Rugolo on The Wrap (archived) (“my 15th birthday in 1979… [I watched] him record the song ‘Get on the Floor.’”); the month and date of Rugolo’s birthday was confirmed by blogger SherylCT in 2010 (archived), who said that, on the day of the lunch for the film “The Dry Land” during the Sundance 2010 Film Festival, “It was [Rugolo’s] birthday”. The date of this “Dry Land” lunch is confirmed on Variety (archived) as January 24, 2010. Since her birthday is January 24, and Michael and his brothers kicked off their “Destiny World Tour” in Germany on January 22, 1979, the studio visit was most likely close to her birthday month, thus mid-January 1979.

 

Mike Smallcombe, “Making Michael”

Once the “Off the Wall” sessions were underway, Michael wrote ‘Get on the Floor’ with musician Louis Johnson, who was performing bass on other tracks. Johnson had been working on a bass idea with his band The Brothers Johnson for their upcoming album Light Up the Night, but it had no melody. Michael heard the bass track and liked it, so Quincy suggested that the pair complete the song, with Michael adding the missing melody.

 

Gina Rugolo, fan, “The Wrap” (June 26, 2009) (archived)

For my 15th birthday in 1979, my dad (jazz arranger Pete Rugolo) called his friend Quincy Jones and asked him if he could introduce us. Quincy mentioned he was just starting to record an album with Michael called "Off the Wall" and invited me to spend the day with him at the recording studio.

I don't think I have ever been more excited to meet anybody in my life. Michael was so sweet to me. He knew I was a big fan and let me watch him record the song "Get on the Floor."

I had another big moment when he invited his friend Karen Carpenter to join us for a while. At the end of the day, he took the temporary cover of the album and signed it "To Gina, much love in the '80s." I was thrilled because I knew I could brag about it for a whole new decade.
