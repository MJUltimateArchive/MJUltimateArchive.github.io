“Childhood” Music Video / Short Film

 

Nick Brandt, director, Black or White Magazine interview (Google translation of German translation)

Black or White Magazine: The first video you made for Michael Jackson was Childhood. How was the first collaboration?

Nick Brandt: Very good. I was very lucky, Michael loved the end result. We shot the video in a forest, in the heart of a natural park, a car park north of Los Angeles. The entire clip was shot in a week. We spent four nights outside in the woods, then two days in the studio to film the flying boats on the Bluescreen. But Michael was not present.

Black or White Magazine: Did Michael take part in the staging of this clip?

Brandt: No. I suggested the concept to him and he liked it. He liked the idea of ​​the forest at night, and the children who wanted to fly away. This is a metaphor for childhood dreams. The idea of ​​always pursuing his dreams touched Michael very much. He gave me the staging and the cut. I showed him the end result and it told him.

Black or White Magazine: What did these videos cost?

Brandt: The most expensive was Earth Song with a budget of $ 2.6 million. Stranger in Moscow cost 1.8 million Cry, I do not remember exactly, but I believe it amounted to about 1 million just as with Childhood.
