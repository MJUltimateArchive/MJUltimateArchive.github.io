But his brothers wanted him to prioritise The Jacksons’ next album, Destiny, the first the group were to write and produce as a whole. John Luongo, mixer, Disco-Disco.com interview

During the mixing of "Shake Your Body Down" by the Jackson's I worked for over 29 hours (!!!) straight with NO BREAK!! When the record was finally done, I had not noticed that we, me and my engineer, Michael Barbiero, had edited so much new tape into the 1/4" reel that when we fast rewound the tape it was extending over 1" past the end of the reel and was in jeopardy of being twisted and destroyed!! Michael saw this, yelled to me and he and I in sync grabbed on reel each with our bare hands to stop them from damaging the tape. The recording was saved but the tape machine got a bit bloody from slicing both our hands. The effort was worth it, the record went #1 on R&B and POP charts and saved the Jacksons’ career. True Story!!

 

Katherine Jackson, “My Family, the Jacksons” (November 1990)

RANDY: We told CBS, “No, no, no, don’t put out ‘Blame It on the Boogie’; put out ‘Shake Your Body (Down to the Ground),’” which Michael and I had written. But CBS loved “Boogie”; also, I think they still didn’t believe in us one hundred percent.

KATHERINE: Happily, “Shake Your Body” became the album’s second single. It did very well, peaking at number seven on Billboard’s pop chart.

RANDY: Actually, that song should have been number one. It sold two million copies, a great figure for a single. But I don’t think pop radio or the press was ready to accept our return just yet.

 

"Black Stars" (May 1979)

As the brothers grew mentally, physically, and creatively, their musical direction took on new heights of achievement. "Michael and I love to work on writing tunes", says Randy, the youngest member of the aggregation. "A lot of times, I'll develop a particular melody and Michael will write the lyrics. Occasionally, we'll all write music and lyrics together."

..."Shake Your Body", the smash disco tune from the "Destiny" LP has proved to its writers, Randy and Michael Jackson that there is a special blend of rhythmic melodies that grabs people by the pulse of their body. Commenting on the highly successful bounce tune, Michael Jackson, the dynamic twenty-year-old lead singer of The Jacksons, says, "When I was living in New York, shooting 'The Wiz' movie, I often visited Studio 54. I noticed what beats made people respond enthusiastically. You know I really love to dance, and nothing pleases me more than for people to enjoy our songs on the dance floor."
