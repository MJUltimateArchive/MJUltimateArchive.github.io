Slash (Guns n' Roses Guitarist)

 

“Vibe” magazine (June/July 1995)

He’s a fucking brilliant entertainer, a complete natural. He’s the only guy I’ve ever met that’s real—for that kind of music. I grew up listening to The Jackson 5. I used to love “Dancing Machine.”

We've been friends for a while, so he just lets me do what I want to do. I get a basic framework, and I just make up my part and they edit it. I wonder sometimes what it’s gonna sound like, [Laughs] but every time, they do a great job. He’s very shrewd. He’s got a great, sarcastic sense of humor. People always ask me, “Is he weird?” Well, he’s different. But I know what it’s like to be weird, growing up in the music business.

I have to admit working with Michael Jackson is different than working with your basic, gritty rock n' roll band. One time when I went to play for Michael, he walked in with Brooke Shields, and there I am with a cigarette in one hand, a bottle of Jack Daniel’s in the other, and my guitar hanging low around my neck. And he doesn’t care. That’s not the way he is, but I don’t have to change for him. He accepts me for what I am.

“Q” magazine (August 1995)

Q: All over the world, the press lapped [rumors about his personal life] up. Why, and how did he do these things, and was he given a fair crack of the whip by the press?

Slash: We've gotten to be friends over the years. He's definitely not what the public perceives him as. He's not a little kid. He may dig little kids, but he's not naive and as child-like as he makes out. He's very sly. He's got a very sarcastic sense of humor. He's a funny guy.

...Q: With all the accusations, did you ever have any doubts about working with him?

Slash: When I was asked to do the record during the middle of all that, I talked to him and he was real secretive and it was real hard to get in touch with him. But I just wanted to let him know that shit happens basically. I know him well enough to know that none of that stuff is really true.

But when they asked me to do it, just for a second I thought, "Oh boy, do I really want to get involved?", and then I thought, "Well, wait a second, there's no way that I'm not going to play with a friend of mine just because he's going through some controversy, so I just went straight there and did it.
